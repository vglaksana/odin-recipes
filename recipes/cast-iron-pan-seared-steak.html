Cast Iron Pan-Seared Steak

Description

Steak is marinated in the fridge for 45-minutes, then quickly pan-seared in a cast iron skillet before it is finished in the oven.

Ingredient

  • 2 (6 ounce) beef top sirloin steaks
  • 2 cups orange juice
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • ½ cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1½ tablespoons steak seasoning (such as Fiesta Brand® Uncle Chris'), or to taste
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • sea salt to taste

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Place steaks side by side in large casserole dish. Add orange juice, cider vinegar, and Worcestershire sauce. Marinate, uncovered, in the refrigerator for 45 minutes.

  2. Step 2

    3. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).

  3. Step 3

    4. Remove casserole dish from refrigerator. Cover steaks with plastic wrap and let allow to come to room temperature, at least 15 minutes.

  4. Step 4

    5. Place steaks on a clean work surface and generously rub with steak seasoning and black pepper.

  5. Step 5

    6. Heat olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over high heat.

  6. Step 6

    7. Cook steaks in the hot skillet until lightly browned on the bottom, 2 1/2 minutes. Flip and cook until browned on the other side and red in the center, about 2 minutes more. Transfer skillet with the steaks to the oven.

  7. Step 7

    8. Bake in the preheated oven until steaks are firm and reddish-pink to lightly pink in the center, 8 to 10 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read from 130 degrees F (54 degrees C) to 140 degrees F (60 degrees C).

  8. Step 8

    9. Remove steaks from oven; season with salt. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving.
